Why Visit Kiruna?

Kiruna is a unique destination that offers an authentic Arctic experience unlike any other. Located 145 kilometers north of the Arctic Circle, it's Sweden's northernmost city and a gateway to some of the most spectacular natural phenomena on Earth.

From September to March, Kiruna becomes one of the best places in the world to witness the Northern Lights (Aurora Borealis). The mesmerizing dance of green, purple, and pink lights across the Arctic sky is a truly magical experience that draws visitors from around the globe.

Kiruna is also home to the indigenous Sami people, offering visitors a chance to learn about their ancient culture, traditions, and reindeer herding way of life. The city itself is undergoing a remarkable transformation as it's being moved building by building to a new location due to mining subsidence, making it a fascinating study in urban planning and resilience.

Whether you're seeking adventure, cultural immersion, or simply the beauty of untouched Arctic wilderness, Kiruna delivers an unforgettable experience in every season.

Top 3 Things to Do in Kiruna

Visit the Ice Hotel

The world-famous Ice Hotel in Jukkasjärvi is rebuilt every year with ice from the Torne River. Marvel at intricate ice sculptures, sleep in a room made entirely of ice, and enjoy drinks at the ice bar.

See Photos

See the Relocated Church

Kiruna Church, one of Sweden's most beautiful buildings, was moved 3 kilometers to its new location in 2016. This remarkable engineering feat preserved the iconic wooden church that's a symbol of the city.

Learn More

Experience Sami Culture

Visit a traditional Sami reindeer herding camp, learn about their ancient way of life, enjoy a meal cooked over an open fire, and for a truly unique experience, try reindeer sledding through the snowy landscapes.

The Famous Ice Hotel

Each year, artists from around the world create a new Ice Hotel with unique rooms, sculptures, and an ice bar. The hotel melts back into the river each spring, making each visit a once-in-a-lifetime experience.
